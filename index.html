CORE COMPONENTS

1. Structural Time Ranges

The core of BC3 is the division of the market into fixed time windows, aligned with the most relevant institutional moments:

Current Day
  • RTH Open
  • London Open
  • US Open
  • US Close
Previous Day
  • RTH
  • Asia Close
  • London Open
  • US Open

Each window generates an acceptance range based on actual price behavior during that period.

2. Static Price

Each range produces a Static Price, representing:

  • The equilibrium point of the period
  • The price level most accepted by the market

The Static Price acts as a rotation axis around which the market decides whether to:

  • Accept
  • Expand
  • Reject

3. Acceptance Area (Fill)

The fill represents the range:

  • Temporary balance zones
  • Compression or distribution areas
  • Operational space for Momentum

Price remaining inside the fill indicates acceptance.

Price moving outside the fill signals potential expansion.

4. Interday Relationship

The overlap between:

  • Current-day ranges
  • Previous-day ranges

allows the assessment of:

  • Structural continuity
  • Context breaks
  • Regime transitions

BC3 makes visible where the market is operating relative to recent memory.

KEY PRINCIPLE

The market does not react to time.

It reacts to how price is accepted within time.

BC3 is designed to make this relationship visible.
